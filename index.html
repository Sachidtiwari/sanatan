About Us

Welcome to our Hindu religious website, a sanctuary for spiritual growth and understanding. Our mission is to spread the teachings of Hinduism and to create a community for devotees and those interested in learning about this ancient and profound tradition.

We are dedicated to sharing the wisdom of the Vedas, Upanishads, and other sacred texts, as well as celebrating the rich festivals and rituals that are an integral part of Hindu life. Join us in exploring the divine, understanding the universe, and discovering the path to moksha (liberation).

Our Deities

Lord Vishnu

The preserver and protector of the universe, Vishnu is known for his incarnations, including Krishna and Rama.

Lord Shiva

Known as the destroyer, Shiva represents transformation and the cycle of creation, preservation, and destruction.

Goddess Lakshmi

The goddess of wealth and prosperity, Lakshmi is worshipped for bringing abundance and good fortune.

Goddess Saraswati

The goddess of knowledge, music, and arts, Saraswati is revered by students and artists alike.

Lord Hanuman

Hanuman is a Hindu god whose shape is half-monkey, half-human. He is the most devoted follower of Prince Rama, so he features a lot in the Ramayana as well as lots of other traditional Hindu stories and artworks. Hanuman has special powers and qualities that he uses to help Rama rescue his wife Sita.

Lord Ganesh

Ganesha or (Ganesh) is the elephant-headed god in Hindu Religion. He is the son of God Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Ganesha is a very popular god in Hindu Religion, and is one of the most worshipped. Hindu tradition states that Ganesha is a god of wisdom, success and good luck.

Goddess Parvati

Parvati is also the goddess of love and devotion, or Kamakshi (the goddess of fertility), abundance and food/nourishment, or Annapurna. She is also the ferocious Mahakali that wields a sword, wears a garland of severed heads, and protects her devotees and destroys all evil that plagues the world and its beings.

Goddess Durga

Durga is believed to unleash her divine wrath against the wicked for the liberation of the oppressed, and entails destruction to empower creation. Durga is seen as a motherly figure and often depicted as a beautiful woman, riding a lion or tiger, with many arms each carrying a weapon and often defeating demons.

Our Celebrated Festivals

Dashain

Dashain is celebrated to mark the goddess Durga's victory over the demon Mahishasura, the son of Mahisi (Buffalo) and a descendant of sage Kashyap. The battle between Durga and Mahishasura lasted for ten days. Since Durga killed Mahishasura, Durga is also called Mahishasuramardini, meaning "The Killer of Mahishasura".

Learn More

Maha Shivratri

According to the Hindu calendar, the festival is observed on the fourteenth day of the first half (night start with darkness - waning) of the lunar month of Phalguna. The festival commemorates the wedding of Shiva and Parvati, and the occasion that Shiva performs his divine dance, called the Tandava.

Learn More

Ram Navami

This festival celebrates the birth of Lord Rama who is an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. This festival is celebrated during the time of March-April (the 9th day of the first month of the Hindu calendar). Lord Rama, who became king of Ayodhya, was known for His exemplary qualities.

Learn More

Chhath

The Chhath festival is celebrated all over the Indian-subcontinent and is of huge significance in the Hindu religion. This festival is celebrated over a period of four days and is pledged to worshipping the Sun as a deity as well as his two wives Sandhya (Dusk) and Usha (Dawn).

Learn More

Tihar

Tihar is the second biggest Nepali festival after Dashain, and is usually allocated a three-day-long national holiday. The festival is considered novel in that it shows reverence to not just the gods, but also to animals such as crows, cows, and dogs that have long-lived alongside humans.

Learn More

Holi

Holi is known as the festival of colours. It is one of the most important festivals in India. Holi is celebrated each year with zeal and enthusiasm in the month of March by followers of the Hindu religion. Those who celebrate this festival, wait for it every year eagerly to play with colours and have delectable dishes.

Learn More

Navratri

Navratri, in Hinduism, major festival held in honour of the divine feminine. Navratri occurs over 9 days during the month of Ashvin, or Ashvina (in the Gregorian calendar, usually September–October). It often ends with the Dussehra (also called Vijayadashami) celebration on the 10th day.

Learn More

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi, in Hinduism, 10-day festival marking the birth of the elephant-headed deity Ganesha, the god of prosperity and wisdom. It begins on the fourth day (chaturthi) of the month of Bhadrapada (August–September), the sixth month of the Hindu calendar.

Explore Our Temples

Shri Pashupatinath Temple

The Pashupatinath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva and located on the banks of the holy Bagmati River in Kathmandu, Nepal, is a timeless spiritual and cultural symbol. The name "Pashupatinath" comes from the words "Pashu", which means "animal" or "living thing", and "Pati", which means "Lord" or "Master".

Learn More

Shri Muktinath Temple

Muktinath is an ancient Vishnu temple located in Mustang, Nepal. The temple of Muktinath, known as 'the lord of liberation', is sacred to both Hindus and Buddhists whom they worship as an abode of Hindu deity Vishnu and Buddhist deity Avalokiteśvara respectively.

Learn More

Janaki Temple

It is constructed in the mixed style of Hindu architecture. It is a three-storied structure and it is made entirely of stone and marble. There are 60 rooms that are decorated with flag of Nepal, colored glass, engravings and Mithila paintings, with beautiful lattice windows and turrets

Learn More

Changu Narayan Temple

This temple is said to be the oldest Hindu temple still in use in the Kathmandu Valley. Built in the two-tiered pagoda style, the main shrine here is guarded on all sides by pairs of real and mythical beasts – elephants, lions, winged lions, and ram-horned griffons.
